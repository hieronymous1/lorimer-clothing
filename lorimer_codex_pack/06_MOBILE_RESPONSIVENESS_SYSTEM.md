# 06_MOBILE_RESPONSIVENESS_SYSTEM

## Highest Priority
Mobile and responsiveness are the most important execution criteria in the project.

## Core Principle
Mobile is not a shrunk desktop.
Mobile is a recomposed editorial sequence.

## Mobile layout rules
- 1 column only for product browsing unless there is a proven reason otherwise
- preserve hierarchy through rhythm, not through columns
- full width or near full width imagery where appropriate
- maintain generous spacing
- avoid cramped text or over compressed groups

## Why 1 column for products
2 columns reads like standard ecommerce.
1 column reads more editorial and premium.

## Home Page on Mobile
Transform desktop compositions into a sequence such as:
- image
- label / text
- image
- section block
- image
- footer

Do not try to preserve asymmetry literally.

## Products Page on Mobile
- single column product flow
- categories spaced clearly
- product image followed by text with clear separation
- editorial divider images should still feel significant

## S/S24 on Mobile
- each look becomes a compact image then text sequence
- reduce overlong gaps
- maintain a deliberate rhythm
- preserve link clarity

## Product Page on Mobile
- image stack first
- meaningful spacing
- text afterwards
- do not place text beside image on small screens

## Mobile spacing targets
- section to section: 64
- internal element spacing: 24
- image to text: 24 or 40 where needed

## Critical failure modes to avoid
- preserving desktop columns on phone
- shrinking everything uniformly
- collapsing whitespace
- dense grids
- tiny captions under oversized image groups
